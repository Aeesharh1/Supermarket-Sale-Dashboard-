# Supermarket Sales Dashboard (Power BI)

A personal practice project analyzing supermarket sales data to uncover patterns in revenue, customer behavior, and product performance using Power BI.


## 📌 Project Overview

This dashboard explores 1,000 customer transactions from a supermarket, breaking down sales by product line, customer type, gender, payment method, and time. The goal was to practice building an interactive, insight-driven dashboard from raw transactional data — going beyond just charts to actually interpret what the numbers were saying.

##  Objectives

- Visualize overall sales performance (revenue, profit, quantity, margin)
- Compare revenue across customer types, gender, and branches
- Identify top and bottom performing product lines
- Spot sales trends and patterns across days and months
- Practice DAX measures, slicers, and dashboard storytelling in Power BI

##  Key Insights

- Total revenue reached **322.97K** across 1,000 customers, generating **15.38K** in profit (4.76% margin)
- **Member** customers contributed **50.85%** of total revenue, outperforming Normal customers
- **Food and beverages** led product line revenue, while **Health and beauty** lagged behind
- **Saturday** recorded the highest sales activity, suggesting stronger weekend shopping behavior
- Daily sales showed **high volatility** with no clear seasonal trend, suggesting short-term factors like promotions and paydays drive demand more than time of year.

##  Tools & Skills Used

- **Power BI** — data modeling, DAX measures, interactive visuals
- **DAX** — calculated measures for profit margin, revenue breakdowns, and KPIs
- **Data visualization** — KPI cards, bar charts, waterfall chart, treemap, pie/donut charts, line chart
- **Dashboard design** — layout planning, slicers, and visual hierarchy

##  Dataset

Sourced from a [Kaggle Supermarket Sales dataset](https://1drv.ms/x/c/e2a3b45f6c0dabc5/IQCoTChBQ_lcTLMPMydyYG3EAY5Yw_Rj0zhwkDZrhGiaucg?e=qsGiy3), containing transactional data including city, customer type, gender, product line, payment method, and sales figures.

##  Challenges & Learnings

The biggest challenges in this project were **dashboard design** and **getting DAX calculations right**. Several visuals were redone more than once, and some measures took a few attempts to get working correctly. Working through these challenges helped build a stronger understanding of how to structure a dashboard and write effective DAX formulas — lessons that stuck more than passively watching tutorials would have.
